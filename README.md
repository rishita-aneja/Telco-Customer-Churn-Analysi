## 🧐 About the Project
I wanted to understand why customers leave a subscription-based service. Using the **IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset**, I analyzed the behavior of **7,043 customers** to find the "red flags" that lead to churn.

This project isn't just about numbers; it's about finding real business solutions to keep customers happy and subscribed.

## 🛠️ Tools I Used
* **Python (VS Code):** Used for data cleaning and initial exploration.
* **Power BI:** Built an interactive dashboard to visualize the "Why" behind the data.
* **Pandas & Seaborn:** For checking correlations and handling missing values.

## 🔍 Key Findings from the Data
After cleaning and analyzing the data, here is what I found:
* **The High-Risk Zone:** Our overall churn rate is **26.54%**. 
* **Contract Impact:** Most of our "churned" customers were on **Month-to-Month** contracts (nearly 55% of all customers use this type). This is the biggest opportunity for retention.
* **Service Gaps:** Customers using **Fiber Optic** internet have higher churn rates than DSL users, suggesting a possible issue with service cost or stability.
* **The "Safety Net":** Customers with **Tech Support** and **Online Security** enabled are significantly more loyal.

## 📈 Dashboard Highlights
I divided my Power BI report into 3 specific pages to make it easy for a manager to read:
1. **Executive Summary:** The big picture—Revenue vs. Churn.
2. **Customer Loyalty:** A breakdown of tenure (how long they stay) and contract types.
3. **Service Audit:** Using a **Waterfall Chart** to show "Revenue Leakage" and a **Scatter Plot** to find our most valuable customers.
